Archytas of Tarentum (c. 400 BC)
Given a = 1 and b = 2, find x,y such that:
  a : x = x : y = y : b
  1 : x = x : y = y : 2
  → x = ∛2 ≈ 1.2599
Construction: intersect three surfaces—
  ■ Cylinder: x² + y² = x
  ■ Cone: x² + y² + z² = (x²/a²)(a² + b²-like)
  ■ Torus: (x²+y²)² = x²+y²
The intersection point P gives ∛2.
This is the first known solution requiring 3D construction — exactly the "motion beyond the straightedge" that the Penman enacts through the 3° Neusis tilt.
